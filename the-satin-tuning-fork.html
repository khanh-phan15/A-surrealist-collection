The Satin Tuning Fork is a painting by Yves Tanguy, filled with unidentifiable, sexually suggestive objects rendered with striking precision. The eerie lighting, long shadows, and deep receding spaces.
Tanguy’s style was rooted in illusionism and the meticulous depiction of the subconscious. His work aligned with the darker, more ominous qualities of Surrealism, reflecting themes of isolation, the unknown, and the irrational. His influence helped broaden the surrealist narrative by infusing it with a sense of the uncanny.
