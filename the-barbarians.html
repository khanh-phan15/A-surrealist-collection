The Barbarians is an example of Max Ernst’s use of surrealist techniques like decalcomania and grattage. These processes allowed him to generate unpredictable textures and forms, which led to works filled with anthropomorphic figures in strange and deserted landscapes.

This artwork exemplifies the Surrealist interest in violence and annihilation, common themes within the movement’s psychological exploration. Ernst’s method of creating spontaneous imagery through these techniques brought Surrealism’s focus on chance and the subconscious to the forefront.
