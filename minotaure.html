Minotaure was a publication that served as a central Surrealist review. Co-edited by Salvador Dalí and André Breton, it acted as a key platform for exploring and disseminating Surrealist ideas.
Minotaure allowed for the exchange of Surrealist concepts between visual artists and writers, contributing to the intellectual foundation of the movement. Its publication cemented the connection between literature and visual art, illustrating Surrealism’s broad influence on culture at large.
